This document outlines the systematic process for reviewing and improving your trading based on the five core building blocks of top-down analysis. Use this to audit your journal entries and identify exactly where your process succeeded or broke down.

---

### **Part 1: Individual Metric Review & Improvement**

#### **1. Bias (The "What" and "Why")**
*   **What to Review:** Examine the **arguments** you stacked. Did you identify a clear "one-sided" market, or were the arguments 50/50?. Check if you correctly identified **respect** (wicks) or **disrespect** (body closes) of high-timeframe PD arrays.
*   **How to Improve:** If your bias is often wrong, stop trying to predict long-term moves and focus on the **next monthly or daily candle**. If a chart isn't clear within 30 seconds, mark it as "No Bias" and move on.
*   **Scenarios:**
    *   *Success:* Bearish arguments are removed because price disrespected a bearish FVG; only bullish arguments remain.
    *   *Failure:* You chose a bullish bias while price was respecting a bearish premium array and showing no bullish candle science.

#### **2. Narrative (The "Path")**
*   **What to Review:** Did the specific PD array you chose (e.g., a Daily FVG) actually **deliver** price to the target?.
*   **How to Improve:** Ensure you are using a narrative that is "closer" to price for higher probability. If the narrative failed, check if it lacked **Red Folder News** or **Time** (volatility) to provide energy.
*   **Scenarios:**
    *   *Success:* Price retraces into a Daily FVG and immediately expands toward the draw on liquidity.
    *   *Failure:* Price ignores your 4h FVG and continues to consolidate because it was a "low-energy" Monday without news.

#### **3. Context Area (The "Where")**
*   **What to Review:** Did price "sting" your lower-timeframe boundary and hold?. Was this area supported by at least **Daily context**?.
*   **How to Improve:** Refine your timeframe selection. If you use a 4h narrative, your context should be a 1h or 15m PD array. If the area is frequently disrespected, you may be picking zones against the HTF flow.
*   **Scenarios:**
    *   *Success:* Price reaches a 15m FVG (your context area) inside a Daily narrative and rejects.
    *   *Failure:* Price closes through your context area with a large body (disrespect), invalidating the setup.

#### **4. Entry (The "Execution Signal")**
*   **What to Review:** Did your specific entry pattern (e.g., 1m FVG or Market Maker Model) form **inside** the context area?.
*   **How to Improve:** If you are missing entries, look at the 1m or 5m timeframes once the context area is hit. If your entries result in immediate stops, ensure you aren't entering before a "counter-argument" is removed.
*   **Scenarios:**
    *   *Success:* A 1m entry confirmation forms precisely as price stings a 15m context area.
    *   *Failure:* Price reaches the target without ever forming your entry pattern, or the pattern forms outside the context zone.

#### **5. Risk Management (The "Safety")**
*   **What to Review:** Did you follow your predefined stop and target?. Did you apply **stricter risk** for shorts on indices or during low-volatility days?.
*   **How to Improve:** Review your PnL relative to your R:R. If you "took partials too early" (as seen in the template), identify if it was due to fear or a genuine "disrespect" candle forming against you.
*   **Scenarios:**
    *   *Success:* You exited the trade manually because a candle disrespected your narrative, saving 50% of the risk.
    *   *Failure:* You moved your stop loss or ignored a news event that increased volatility beyond your risk tolerance.

### **Part 2: Combined Metric Scenarios**

To improve your journal's "Data Import," use these combinations to diagnose the "health" of your trading system.

| Combination (Bias / Narrative / Context / Entry) | Outcome & Review Instruction |
| :--- | :--- |
| **YES / YES / YES / YES** | **The "Perfect" Trade:** Everything aligned. Review for efficiency—could you have had a better entry or R:R?. |
| **YES / YES / NO / NO** | **The "Left Without Me" Trade:** You were right about direction and path, but price didn't pull back to your zone. **Improvement:** Look for "closer" PD arrays or smaller timeframes for context. |
| **YES / YES / YES / NO** | **The "Missed Signal":** Analysis was perfect, but the entry pattern didn't form. **Improvement:** This is a "good" miss. Do not force an entry next time. |
| **YES / NO / NO / NO** | **The "Vague Analysis" Trade:** You got the direction right, but your path and zone were wrong. **Improvement:** You likely traded based on "intuition" rather than objective arguments. |
| **NO / NO / YES / YES** | **The "Lucky" Trade:** You entered a zone that worked despite bad HTF analysis. **Improvement:** This is dangerous. Review why your bias was wrong to avoid "luck-based" habits. |
| **YES / YES / NO / YES** | **The "Force-Fed" Trade:** You took an entry even though the context area was disrespected or never hit. **Improvement:** Discipline check. You must wait for the "boundary" to be reached. |
| **NO / NO / NO / NO** | **The "System Failure":** Complete breakdown. **Improvement:** Return to the Monthly/Weekly timeframe. You are likely lost in the "noise" of lower timeframes. |

### **Combined Risk Management Scenarios**
*   **All Metrics YES / Risk NO:** You had a winning setup but lost money or broke even. **Review:** This is an execution/psychology issue, not an analytical one.
*   **All Metrics NO / Risk YES:** You lost the trade but followed your plan. **Review:** This is a "good loss." Your risk management protected your capital while the market provided a learning opportunity.
